This project explores the idea of creating a tangible digital interface, rescuing people from their daily interaction with small,
smaller and tinier screens.Todayâ€™s technology deserves due credit for enhancing our lives drastically. We started off as mere
cave dwellers but today we can envision a future in a completely different planet all thanks to the growing technology around
us. Unfortunately, this rapid growth has neglected many key factors that are slowly starting haunt us.
Today our interactions are vastly limited to just touching a block of glass, over time turning humans into a static
non-active being. If we notice, these technological interactions are also limited only to the fully capable and neglects
those with disabilities. I believe a key priority for technology is to enhance the lives of those with disabilities
rather than excluding them from the system, alienating them further from the community. Sentient aims at
taking human-digital interactions to its next step by allowing us to actively interact with technology and making it
accessible to various groups of people essentially making them one.

This form of interaction uses Posenet as a foundation and builds upon its capabilities, allowing our physical movements
to trigger digital commands. This creates a digital space where information is given a coordinate allowing us to tap the
z-axis of interaction, helping us move away from our glass blocks. This method of interaction helps to personalize
interactions and tune the system to custom triggers. For example a person with limited motor skills can have a model
trained to his scope of movements and the digital commands adapt to those interactions, creating a seamless way of
interacting with technology for the disabled. My goal with this method of interaction is to push digital interactions to
its next phase optimizing the way we access and utilize information to push humans to its next phase, from being just
human to sentient.
